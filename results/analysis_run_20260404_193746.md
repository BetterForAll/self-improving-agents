## What Each Level Adds

**Level 1 - AutoResearch**: This basic loop successfully demonstrated the feasibility of LLM-driven code improvement. It provided significant initial gains across all tasks (2.0x to 291.0x improvement) for remarkably low costs, highlighting that even simple iterative refinement can yield substantial benefits, especially when starting from a low baseline.

**Level 2 - Feedback Loop**: By introducing a structured reviewer, this level enabled more targeted and effective code refinement. It achieved the highest raw performance scores for `snake` and `support`, indicating that specific feedback with an issue taxonomy can guide the LLM to generate higher-quality and more optimized solutions for complex problems.

**Level 3 - HyperAgent**: The meta-agent capable of rewriting its own source code aimed for deeper self-improvement. While it showed a remarkable 13.3x improvement for `support` from its lower baseline and good performance on `snake`, it did not consistently outperform the Feedback Loop in terms of absolute "Best" scores. Its complexity did not always translate to superior outcomes compared to structured feedback, though it did achieve 100% on original `email_validation` tests.

**Level 4 - Arena Loop**: This level introduced adversarial testing and tournament selection, proving crucial for solution robustness. While often exhibiting lower "Best" scores and significantly higher costs per iteration, the data for `email_validation` explicitly demonstrates that Arena Loop produced the most robust solution. Its value lies in building resilience against evolving and adversarial conditions rather than raw peak performance on a fixed benchmark.

## Cross-Validation Insight

The cross-validation data for `email_validation` provides a critical insight: levels 1-3, while achieving high or even 100% accuracy on their original fixed test suite, produced brittle solutions that failed sharply when exposed to an expanded set of adversarial test cases. For example, AutoResearch and HyperAgent scored 100% on 20 original tests but only 62% on the 50 expanded tests. In contrast, the Arena Loop, which uses an evolving benchmark, achieved the highest combined score of 84%, demonstrating genuine robustness. This proves that relying on a fixed benchmark often leads to agents that "game" the test suite rather than producing genuinely reliable and generalizable solutions. An evolving benchmark, as employed by the Arena Loop, is essential for truly robust self-improvement.

## Cost-Effectiveness

AutoResearch is highly cost-effective for initial gains, particularly for `email_validation` (1052.6x per $) and `snake` (4157.1x per $). Feedback Loop shows strong cost-effectiveness for `snake` (7052.1x per $) and `support` (67.2x per $), offering substantial improvements for moderate cost. HyperAgent is notably cost-effective for `support` (212.5x per $). Arena Loop consistently exhibits the lowest improvement per dollar across all tasks (e.g., 8.1x per $ for `email_validation`, 6.4x per $ for `support`). This highlights diminishing returns as complexity and robustness increase; the high cost of Arena Loop is justified by its unique ability to produce robust, non-brittle solutions, rather than raw efficiency on a static metric.

## When to Use Which Level

For quick, low-cost initial improvements on well-defined metrics, **AutoResearch** is highly effective. If problems require more targeted refinement and better peak performance, **Feedback Loop** offers a good balance of efficacy and cost-effectiveness. **HyperAgent** may be considered for tasks that could significantly benefit from fundamental changes to the agent's internal logic, although its returns on raw performance metrics are not always superior to Feedback Loop. For critical applications where robustness against adversarial or unseen conditions is paramount and cost is a secondary concern, **Arena Loop** is the superior choice, as it is uniquely designed to produce genuinely reliable solutions by evolving the benchmark itself.
